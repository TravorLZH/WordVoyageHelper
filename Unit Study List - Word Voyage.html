Unit Name - January Words 1

Print

abide
Definition: 1.) To endure without yielding; to bear patiently; to accept without objection; to put up with. 2.) To wait; to pause; to delay. 3.) To stay; to continue in a place; to have one's abode; to dwell; with with before a person, and commonly with at or in before a place. 4.) To remain stable or fixed in some state or condition; to continue; to remain.
Sample sentence: You must abide by the rules of the house.
acquiesce
Definition: To rest satisfied, or apparently satisfied, or to rest without opposition and discontent (usually implying previous opposition or discontent); to accept or consent by silence; to concur.
Sample sentence: Our teacher decided to acquiesce to our pleas to hold class outside.
adjuration
Definition: 1.) An earnest request; entreaty. 2.) A solemn or desperate urging or counseling.
Sample sentence: I could not resist my sister's adjuration to sing at her wedding.
apprehension
Definition: 1.) Anticipation of things unfavorable; distrust or fear at the prospect of future evil. 2.) The act of grasping with the intellect, especially intuitive understanding; perception; the acceptance of information, without passing judgment on its validity. 3.) The act of seizing or taking by legal process; arrest.
Sample sentence: I watched the weather forecast with growing apprehension.
barricade
Definition: 1.) Any barrier that obstructs passage; obstacle. 2.) A fortification or obstruction made in haste, as in a street, to obstruct the progress of an enemy.
Sample sentence: The police barricade was no match for the crush of frenzied fans.
chiffonier
Definition: A movable and ornamental closet or piece of furniture with shelves or drawers.
Sample sentence: My grandmother kept her favorite scarves in the top drawer of the chiffonier.
compulsory
Definition: 1.) Obligatory; mandatory; necessary. 2.) Compelling; constraining.
Sample sentence: The study of a foreign language is compulsory in our school.
congregation
Definition: 1.) An assembly; a gathering; esp. an assembly of persons met for religious worship. 2.) The act of bringing together, or of collecting into one aggregate or mass; the state of being gathered.
Sample sentence: The congregation voted to hire a carpenter to build a new steeple.
constable
Definition: 1.) An officer of the peace having police and minor judicial functions is a small town. 2.) A high officer in the monarchical establishments of the Middle Ages.
Sample sentence: The local constable made sure no one at the pub became too unruly.
consternation
Definition: Alarming amazement or dread that results in utter confusion; dismay.
Sample sentence: We looked at each other with consternation when we heard the new assignment.
countenance
Definition: 1.) Appearance, especially the look or expression of the face. 2.) Calm facial expression; composure. 3.) Approval; encouragement; moral support.
Sample sentence: His grim countenance softened when he watched his children play.
crevice
Definition: A narrow opening resulting from a split or crack; a cleft; a fissure; a rift.
Sample sentence: The mice were slipping into the cellar through a narrow crevice in the foundation.
declaration
Definition: 1.) The act of publicly announcing. 2.) A positive, explicit or formal statement; proclamation. 3.) That which is announced or proclaimed; the document containing such a statement.
Sample sentence: His declaration of love was followed by a proposal of marriage.
defiance
Definition: 1.) A daring or bold resistance to authority or to any opposing force. 2.) Open disregard; contempt. 3.) A challenge.
Sample sentence: Any defiance of the emperor's authority was met with severe punishment.
delusional
Definition: Having false or unrealistic beliefs or opinions.
Sample sentence: Your plans to become a professional basketball player seem delusional to me.
derive
Definition: 1.) To receive or obtain, as from a source or origin; in chemistry, to obtain or produce (one substance) from another, actually or theoretically. 2.) To come from a source or origin; originate. 3.) To trace from a source or origin. 4.) To reach or obtain by reasoning; deduce; infer.
Sample sentence: Your parents derive great pleasure from seeing you succeed.
dexterity
Definition: 1.) Skill and ease in using the hands or body; agility. 2.) Mental quickness and skill; cleverness.
Sample sentence: The street juggler's dexterity amazed the gathering crowd.
disconsolate
Definition: 1.) Without comfort or solace; hopelessly unhappy; that cannot be comforted. 2.) Characterized by or causing dejection; cheerless; gloomy.
Sample sentence: Jesse became disconsolate when his dog was still missing after three days.
emphatic
Definition: 1.) Uttered or marked with special stress laid upon, or importance attached to, anything. 2.) Forceful; insistent. 3.) Very impressive or significant; striking; clearly or boldly outlined.
Sample sentence: His response to the petition was immediate and emphatic.
endeavor
Definition: To exert physical or intellectual strength for the attainment of; to use efforts to effect; to strive to achieve or reach; to try; to attempt.
Sample sentence: I will endeavor to make my Halloween candy last until Thanksgiving.
exertion
Definition: The act of putting into motion or action; the active exercise of any power or faculty; an effort, esp. a laborious or vigorous effort.
Sample sentence: On hot steamy days like this, I try to avoid any unnecessary exertion.
exhibitionist
Definition: 1.) A person who behaves in ways intended to attract attention or display his or her powers, personality, etc. 2.) Psychiatry: a person afflicted with a disorder characterized especially by a compulsion to exhibit the genitals in public.
Sample sentence: My cousin is a bit of an exhibitionist and loves to wear wild clothes and crazy hair.
exultant
Definition: Rejoicing triumphantly; highly related; jubilant.
Sample sentence: The exultant football team carried their coach on their shoulders.
faintly
Definition: In a manner hardly perceptible, lacking strength, brightness, clarity, loudness, etc.; feebly.
Sample sentence: The hot breeze smelled faintly of strawberries.
falsetto
Definition: 1.) A false or artificially high voice, especially in a man. 2.) A person, especially a man, who signs with such a voice.
Sample sentence: The clown sang opera in a falsetto while juggling.
grave
Definition: 1.) Threatening a seriously bad outcome; critical. 2.) Serious or solemn; somber. 3.) Weighty or important.
Sample sentence: The carpenter's careless attitude could have grave consequences when a family moves into the house.
halitosis
Definition: A condition of having bad-smelling breath.
Sample sentence: Eating lots of raw onions on your sandwich may give you halitosis.
horizontal
Definition: 1.) At right angles to the vertical; parallel to level ground. 2.) Flat or level.
Sample sentence: The hillside was covered with a series of horizontal terraces to catch the rain.
impel
Definition: 1.) To drive or urge forward; to press on; to incite to action or motion in any way. 2.) To cause to move forward; propel; impart motion to.
Sample sentence: Rising sea levels may one day impel communities to move inland.
incredulous
Definition: Indicating or showing unbelief; skeptical.
Sample sentence: When I told her my grades, she gave me an incredulous smile.
inquisitive
Definition: 1.) Given to investigation, asking questions or research; intellectually curious; eager for knowledge. 2.) Unduly or inappropriately curious; prying.
Sample sentence: The inquisitive ape quickly figured out how to open the door.
interlocutor
Definition: 1.) One who takes part in a conversation or dialogue, sometimes as a go-between. 2.) A man in the middle of the line in a minstrel show who questions the end men and acts as the announcer. 3.) A person who questions; interrogator.
Sample sentence: During contract negotiations, the union sent proposals to the company through an interlocutor.
interpose
Definition: 1.) To place between. 2.) To thrust; to intrude; to put between, either for aid or for troubling. 3.) To introduce or inject between the parts of a conversation or argument.
Sample sentence: I rushed to interpose myself between the child and my exuberant dog.
lagoon
Definition: 1.) A shallow sound, channel, pond, or lake, connected to a larger body of water, especially one into which the sea flows. 2.) An artificial pool for storage and treatment of polluted or excessively hot sewage, industrial waste, etc.
Sample sentence: Just behind the beach was a quiet lagoon where we made our campsite.
monotonous
Definition: 1.) Uttered in one unvarying tone; continued with dull uniformity; without change or variety; wearisome. 2.) Characterizing a sound continuing on one note.
Sample sentence: The speaker's voice was so monotonous that I found myself falling asleep.
penitent
Definition: Feeling or expressing sorrow on account of sins or offenses and resolved on atonement and amendment of life.
Sample sentence: The penitent knight vowed to spend the rest of his life in service to others.
prodigious
Definition: 1.) Extraordinary in bulk, extent, quantity, or degree; very great; vast; huge; immense. 2.) Marvelous; wonderful; portentous; of momentous or ominous significance.
Sample sentence: Adding plant food to the soil resulted in prodigious growth in the garden.
prophesy
Definition: 1.) To foretell; to predict; to prognosticate. 2.) To herald; to indicate beforehand.
Sample sentence: Madame Louise claims she can prophesy your future romances.
psychoanalyze
Definition: To investigate or treat by investigating unconscious mental processes.
Sample sentence: My brother is always trying to psychoanalyze me to explain my every action.
relinquish
Definition: 1.) To let go or release; to leave behind; to abandon; to quit. 2.) To give up; to renounce or surrender.
Sample sentence: When Mary moved, she had to relinquish her position as class president.
remonstrance
Definition: 1.) The act of saying or pleading in protest or disapproval. 2.) A protest.
Sample sentence: Club members wrote a letter of remonstrance about rising membership fees.
remorse
Definition: The anguish, like gnawing pain, excited by regret for wrongdoing.
Sample sentence: Her posture in the courtroom showed her deep remorse.
salutation
Definition: The act of saluting, or paying respect or reverence, by customary words or actions; the act of greeting, or expressing good will or courtesy; also, that which is uttered or done in saluting or greeting.
Sample sentence: We received a hearty salutation when we walked into the pub.
sarcastic
Definition: Expressing, or expressed by, harsh irony; characterized by, or of the nature of, a cutting remark; given to the use of harsh irony; bitterly satirical; scornfully severe; taunting.
Sample sentence: Although we are very good friends, sometimes we make sarcastic comments about each other.
scrutinize
Definition: To examine closely; to inspect or observe with critical attention.
Sample sentence: It is wise to scrutinize the details of any contract before you sign it.
smoldering
Definition: 1.) Burning slowly without flame, usually emitting smoke 2.) Being in a state of suppressed activity. 3.) Displaying repressed feelings, as of indignation, anger or the like.
Sample sentence: A smoldering cigarette started a huge forest fire.
successor
Definition: A person or thing that follows; one who takes the place which another has left, and sustains the like part or character.
Sample sentence: After I left my job, my successor changed all of my policies.
trepidation
Definition: 1.) Tremulous terror or alarm; fear; confusion; fright; agitation; perturbation. 2.) Trembling or quivering movement; tremor.
Sample sentence: We opened the door into the haunted house with trepidation.
vestry
Definition: 1.) A room attached to a church, in which ceremonial garments and sacred objects are sometimes kept, and where meetings for worship or parish business are held; a sacristy. 2.) A committee elected by members of the church to manage its temporal concerns.
Sample sentence: After the service, the choir returned their robes to the vestry.
virtuous
Definition: Conforming to moral and ethical principles; morally excellent; upright; chaste.
Sample sentence: The princess is seeking a virtuous prince to rule by her side.
